# Day 14 - Flow Governance

## Approval Workflow Examples

Approval workflows are used when records require authorization before proceeding.

### Examples

### Student Scholarship Approval
1. Student submits scholarship request.
2. Request is sent to the faculty.
3. Faculty reviews and approves/rejects.
4. Student receives notification.

### Course Creation Approval
1. New course is created.
2. Department Head reviews the request.
3. Approval is granted before the course becomes active.

---

## Branching Flow Logic

Branching logic allows a Flow to follow different paths based on conditions.

### Example

If Attendance >= 75%
→ Student is eligible for exams.

Else
→ Warning notification is sent.

### Another Example

If Course Seats Available
→ Registration Approved.

Else
→ Registration Rejected.

### Benefits
- Automates decision making
- Reduces manual work
- Improves efficiency

## Governance Explanation

Governance is the set of rules, policies, and controls used to manage business processes.

### Why Governance Matters
- Ensures compliance with business rules
- Protects sensitive data
- Reduces operational risks
- Improves accountability
- Maintains data quality

### Governance in Salesforce
- Approval Processes
- Validation Rules
- User Permissions
- Record Access Controls
- Audit Tracking

---

## Reflection

Flow governance helps organizations automate processes while maintaining control and security. Approval workflows, branching logic, and governance policies ensure that business operations are accurate, secure, and compliant with organizational requirements.

## Revision Questions

### 1. Why are approval workflows important?
They ensure actions are reviewed before being approved.

### 2. Why do businesses require governance?
To control processes, security, and compliance.

### 3. What are branching workflows?
Workflows that follow different paths based on conditions.

### 4. Why should automation follow business rules?
To ensure correct and compliant operations.

### 5. Why are decision nodes important in flows?
They determine which path the flow should follow.

### 6. Why should enterprises restrict sensitive operations?
To protect data and prevent unauthorized actions.

### 7. Why are approvals important in large organizations?
They provide accountability and reduce risks.

### 8. Why should workflows be auditable?
To track actions and support compliance requirements.

## Reflection

Flow governance helps organizations automate processes while maintaining control and security. Approval workflows, branching logic, and governance policies ensure that business operations are accurate, secure, and compliant with organizational requirements.

### Why do enterprises require controlled workflows instead of unrestricted actions?

Enterprises require controlled workflows to ensure that all business processes follow defined rules and approval procedures. Controlled workflows help prevent errors, unauthorized changes, security risks, and compliance issues. They also improve accountability, transparency, and consistency across the organization. By using approvals and governance controls, enterprises can protect data, reduce risks, and maintain reliable business operations.
